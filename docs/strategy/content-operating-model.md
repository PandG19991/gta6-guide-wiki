# Content Operating Model

## Search Demand Buckets

- Release and buying: release date, preload, preorder, price, platform, PC status.
- Map and world: map, Leonida, Vice City, locations, regions, interactive map.
- Database: cars, vehicles, weapons, characters, missions, businesses, safehouses.
- Launch help: beginner guide, best settings, money, early weapons, police escape.
- Evergreen: 100 percent checklist, trophies, collectibles, secrets, side activities.

## Source Priority

1. Rockstar official pages, Newswire, and media pages.
2. Take-Two investor or official publisher announcements.
3. PlayStation, Xbox, and official store listings.
4. Hands-on testing after release.
5. Trusted media interviews or previews.
6. Community maps and wiki pages as leads only.
7. Rumors or leaks stay out of main guide/database pages.

## Update Cadence

- Pre-launch: check official, store, and Take-Two pages daily during marketing beats.
- Launch week: update hands-on pages several times per day, but only after verification.
- Post-launch: update map/database/checklists weekly until complete.

## Page Quality Gate

Each page needs:

- a clear answer or task outcome;
- source links or a confidence label;
- last updated date;
- original explanation or testing notes;
- internal links to the related hub/database page;
- no copied text, no fake facts, no leaked assets.

## Monetization Gate

Do not apply for or add AdSense until:

- the pages selected for monetization are complete, useful, and free of placeholder content;
- privacy/contact/editorial/source pages are current;
- the publisher account and applicable consent behavior are ready;
- ads can be placed away from navigation, filters, and interactive controls;
- the media-use decision and replacement path are documented.

There is no fixed page-count or traffic threshold. Apply when the product and policy boundary are ready, then use a bounded low-density experiment to measure impact.
